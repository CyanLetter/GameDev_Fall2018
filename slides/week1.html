Game Design Process

A general overview - experiences vary.

Gunpoint

1. Research

What exists, what's possible, what's missing.

2. Specs

  • Target Audience
  • Platform

3. Ideation

  • Story (outline, key points)
  • Mechanics (reference existing or describe new)
  • Look & Feel (mood board, sketch)

4. Prototype

  • Ugly playable example of game mechnics
  • Sketches and style frames

ARMS (7:12)

Lucky's Tale

5a. Planning

User flow

  • Levels, Scope
  • Onboarding
  • Menus & UI
  • Cutscenes, Ending
  • Sound Design

5b. Planning

Mechanics

  • Lock down core mechanic
  • Secondary mechanics (buttons, scoring, rewards, NPCs)

6. Organize

  • Create task list
  • Prioritize work
  • Assign Tasks
  • Establish schedule for checkin and review

7. Alpha

  • Playable demo - basic game mechanic with placeholder art
  • Internal quality control
  • Playtesting

8. Beta

  • Minimum Viable Product
  • Feature Complete
  • No showstopper bugs or major implementation left.
  • External QC

9. Release Candidate

"We think it's ready to ship"

10. Ship it

Game Design Principles

A few important things to keep in mind.

#1

Focus on a core game mechanic

The most fundamental player actions should work well and be fun.

#2

Easy to learn, difficult to master.

Make your mechanics broadly accessible, but ensure there is also depth to reward continued play.

#3

Meaningful Play

A much debated term. Generally, make sure your game has a 'why', a reason for players to care about your game.

#4

Reward & punish the player appropriately

Challenges should be rewarded with things that expand gameplay - new story, mechanics, environments.

Plenty more, like balance, pacing, atmosphere, but these are a solid foundation.
